Flow calibration — NTC temperature vs flow rate

The core flow-rate curve (l/min through the pipe bore). Air and still-water levels are drawn as reference bands; the marker is the current flow rate. This monotonic curve is what the firmware inverts to read flow.

Turn-on transient — NTC vs time (cold start, self-regulating PTC)

Each medium has a distinct time constant — a second, independent discriminator the firmware can use alongside the steady reading.

Cool-down transient — NTC vs time (heater switched OFF)

Starting from the hot steady state, the heater is turned off and the probe decays toward fluid temperature. With no heater power the rate is set purely by convection and thermal mass: flowing water drops fastest, air stays hot longest (a persistent dry signature). The decay is heater-block-mass dominated — raise Simulation time to see the full curve.

Flow-step response — how fast the sensor reacts to a flow change

The probe is at steady state for the "from" flow, then the flow changes to the "to" value over the configurable ramp time (0 = instantaneous step). The heater stays on (self-regulating), so only the head and shaft re-equilibrate. Set the from/to rates and ramp time in the Flow-step section. Dashed lines are the before/after steady readings; the markers show the 63% response time and the moment the flow ramp ends.

Heat flux removed vs flow rate

Power carried away from the head as flow increases.

Thresholds vs starting water temperature (5–30 °C)

How the regime readings and detection thresholds shift as the fluid temperature varies. Straight lines ⇒ a simple linear temperature compensation works — the ESP32 can read ambient by briefly switching the heater off. The marker is the current fluid temperature.

Sensor signal vs flow — NTC divider voltage

The voltage the ADC actually sees (NTC + series resistor), with the ±2σ ADC-noise band. The curve flattens badly at high flow, so the noise band there spans a wide range of flows — that overlap is the resolution limit. Set NTC/ADC values in the NTC & ADC section.

Flow-rate accuracy — ±1σ uncertainty vs flow

ADC noise propagated through the calibration slope. A small temperature (hence resistance, hence voltage) change per l/min gives a large flow uncertainty — worst at high flow. Improve it with phosphor bronze / the Al sleeve (bigger temperature swing), a series resistor matched to the flow-region resistance, or more ADC averaging.
